Pilot

vita motu constat (or "life is in constant movement") - This quote has impacted me for sometime now.

I am 24 as I write this presentation text and so far I have moved 20 times in my life, between cities, houses, and most importantly across Brazil and the around the world. So... life has been literally about moving.

I believe we weren't ever meant to last, but to constantly become anew, everywhere we go and everyday. Despite cliche, being essential, intentional and mindful are key to who I am and want to become as I put myself up to the challenge of perhaps leaving a mark of (hopefully positive) impact in people lives is what inspires me in all I do.

Nevertheless, as much as reality allows (regarding the system we live in as well as what must be done to keep one's existence), I try to keep the "essentialism" vibe when it comes to deciding what I put my energy on and (more importantly) what I don't. Creating this blog is something I wanted to put my energy for a long time now, but never did, so I decided to stop wasting time and energy on some other useless things in my life and look a bit inside.

And that's all this blog is all about, looking inside me. Of course there will be technical stuff here for the nerd that I am, but I have been writing for myself on diaries since ever, drafting ideas and coming up with meditations on so many things, so why not share a bit?

I don't mean even for a second to be presumptions by assuming people want to know about what I think, but I believe that it will be good for my own sake to store those things somewhere that doesn't get lost in a drawer like a notebook or lost in the fading of ink. Besides that, I have heard I helped people at least once with conversations and perspectives, so maybe if I am lucky enough, there might some use to the gibberish that crosses my brain.

For some reason writing in english is easier for me, perhaps because it gets less personal, or more romantic hahaha but I will for sure write both in portuguese and english here and I will try to post always both versions.
